# 📊 Customer Shopping Behaviour Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes customer shopping behavior in a retail/e-commerce environment to uncover insights about purchasing patterns, customer segmentation, and revenue generation. The goal is to help businesses make data-driven decisions to improve sales, marketing strategies, and customer retention.
## 🎯 Business Objectives
* Analyze revenue contribution across different customer segments
* Evaluate the impact of discounts on purchasing behavior
* Identify top-performing products based on ratings and sales
* Compare spending patterns based on shipping type and subscription status
* Segment customers into New, Returning, and Loyal categories

## 📂 Dataset Information
* **Dataset Name:** Customer Shopping Pattern Dataset
* **Total Rows:** 3,900
* **Total Columns:** 18
### Key Features:
* Customer ID, Age, Gender
* Item Purchased, Category
* Purchase Amount (USD), Discount Applied
* Subscription Status, Shipping Type
* Review Rating, Previous Purchases
* Payment Method, Purchase Frequency

## 🧹 Data Preprocessing
* Checked for missing values and handled inconsistencies
* Removed duplicate records (if any)
* Converted data types where necessary
* Prepared dataset for analysis

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* Performed univariate, bivariate, and multivariate analysis
* Analyzed customer demographics and spending patterns
* Studied impact of discounts, shipping type, and subscriptions
* Identified trends in product performance and ratings

## 🔍 Key Insights
* Subscribed customers tend to spend more than non-subscribers
* Loyal customers contribute the highest revenue
* Discounts increase purchase frequency but not always revenue
* High-rated products are more frequently purchased
* Express shipping users often have higher purchase amounts

## 📈 Tools & Technologies Used

## 📌 Conclusion
The analysis provides valuable insights into customer behavior, helping businesses optimize marketing strategies, improve customer retention, and maximize revenue.
